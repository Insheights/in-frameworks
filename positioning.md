# IIVCS Framework Positioning

Positioning statements for the three IIVCS frameworks. Each states what the framework is, how it relates to the others up and down the system, what it owns, and its half of the dual-track behavioral measurement that distinguishes the IIVCS.

The three are deliberately symmetric: **OSM governs, PTS operationalizes, CVDM realizes** — and feedback runs upstream, CVDM → PTS → OSM. Outcome definition flows down (OSM defines value-stream outcomes, PTS owns their delivery, product teams own the customer outcomes that evidence them); outcome evidence flows up.

---

## OSM — Organization Strategy Model
*Positioning*

OSM is the strategy and governance layer of the IIVCS — the control plane through which an organization sets strategic intent and governs execution by value stream. It translates Values, Mission, and Vision — informed by internal and external context, stakeholder and customer expectations, leadership direction, and enterprise risk — into strategic value priorities and the value-stream outcomes the business intends to produce.

OSM is the top of the system, and its accountability runs in two directions:

- **It governs down to execution.** OSM sets the value-stream outcomes and priorities that PTS builds the operating model to deliver and that CVDM realizes at the customer journey. It governs *what* the organization creates value on and *where* — it does not operate the model or deliver to the customer.
- **It reconciles the return path.** OSM is where feedback resolves into strategic reassessment — PTS's executability signal, CVDM's customer reality, the dual-track AER, and the 8 Values organizational outcomes converge in Governance & Measurement to confirm, refute, or retune strategic hypotheses.

OSM owns **strategic value priorities and the definition of value-stream outcomes** — the business intent each value stream exists to produce — through the governance it exercises. These outcomes are defined here and delivered downstream: OSM defines them, PTS owns their delivery, and customer outcomes evidence them.

OSM is where the system's behavioral and value signals **become strategic decisions**. It does not generate a behavioral track of its own; it is the reconciliation point where Organizational AER (from PTS) and Customer AER (from CVDM), expressed through the 8 Values, validate whether strategic priorities are generating the intended organizational value. Its primary generated value is Strategic Value, and it sets the direction for Innovation Value.

| Relation | OSM's role | Down | Up |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OSM ↔ PTS** | Governs | Sets value-stream outcomes and priorities | Receives executability + organizational readiness (Org AER, People/Operational Value) |
| **OSM ↔ CVDM** | Governs | Sets the customer value priorities the journey must serve | Receives strategic-evolution feedback — customer reality refining strategy |

## PTS — Product Transformation System
*Positioning*

PTS is the product operating model layer of the IIVCS — the system through which an organization structures the value-stream operating model and empowers the persistent cross-functional teams that carry strategy into delivery. It comprises the POM Enablers (Leadership; Practices & Behaviors; Organizational Architecture; Culture & Relationships), the cross-functional teams those enablers empower, and the product management lifecycle they run — all underpinned by Product Mindset.

PTS sits between strategy and delivery, and carries a specific accountability in each direction:

- **It aligns up to OSM.** OSM owns Governance & Execution by value stream and sets the value-stream outcomes the business intends. PTS receives those governed priorities and builds the operating model to deliver them — and returns the *executability* signal: whether the strategy can actually be operationalized through the teams and capabilities that exist. A strategy OSM can articulate but PTS cannot carry is a measurable failure, and it surfaces here.
- **It enables down to CVDM.** The teams and capabilities PTS builds are what CVDM activates to realize value as customer outcomes. PTS does not deliver to the customer; it builds the system that does, and receives CVDM's operational feedback on whether that system performs at the delivery layer.

PTS owns **value-stream outcomes** — business-defined, OSM-governed strategic outcomes — through the value-stream ownership it establishes. The persistent cross-functional **product teams own customer outcomes**, realized through CVDM. Customer outcomes roll up to the value-stream outcome as *evidence* that it is being produced; they do not *define* it. The value-stream outcome is set by the business and proven by customer outcomes — not constituted by their sum.

PTS is where **organizational behavioral change is driven and measured**. As the operating model itself, it is the home of **Organizational AER** — whether teams adopt, engage with, and sustain the way of working — the organizational half of the dual-track behavioral measurement that distinguishes the IIVCS; CVDM holds the customer half (Customer AER). The two run in parallel because organizational value requires both: an operating model that is adopted but produces no customer outcome creates nothing, and customer delivery without a sustained operating model cannot last.

| Relation | PTS's role | Down | Up |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PTS ↔ OSM** | Aligns | Receives governed value-stream priorities | Returns executability (Org AER, People/Operational Value) |
| **PTS ↔ CVDM** | Enables | Supplies empowered teams + capabilities | Receives operational feedback on operating-model performance |

## CVDM — Customer Value Delivery Model
*Positioning*

CVDM is the delivery and realization layer of the IIVCS — the model through which value is realized as a customer outcome. It maps each customer journey step to the value-stream stage that serves it, defining the Products/Services, Capabilities, Processes, and Information flows required to deliver the value proposition, and surfaces signal through five continuous feedback loops.

CVDM is the bottom of the execution stack, and its accountability runs in two directions:

- **It aligns up to PTS.** CVDM is activated by the persistent cross-functional teams and capabilities PTS builds; it is where those capabilities meet the customer journey. It returns operational feedback — delivery learning and performance optimization — telling PTS whether the operating model performs at the delivery layer, and customer reality upward to OSM as strategic-evolution signal.
- **It faces the customer.** CVDM's "down" is the customer and the market themselves — the point where value either lands as a realized outcome or fails to, and where that result is captured as signal.

CVDM owns the **realization model and its measurement** — the journey-to-value mapping and the feedback loops through which customer value is produced and proven. The persistent cross-functional **product teams operating within CVDM own the customer outcomes**; CVDM is the discipline and the measurement through which they realize them. Those customer outcomes evidence the value-stream outcomes PTS owns and OSM defined.

CVDM is where **customer behavioral change is measured**. It is the home of **Customer AER** — whether customers adopt, engage with, and retain the value offered — the customer half of the dual-track behavioral measurement that distinguishes the IIVCS; PTS holds the organizational half (Organizational AER). CVDM is also where 3E is primarily surfaced: Efficiency, Experience, and Effectiveness are measured at the journey and value-stage level and returned upstream.

| Relation | CVDM's role | Down | Up |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CVDM ↔ PTS** | Realizes | Activates the teams + capabilities PTS builds | Returns operational feedback (delivery learning, performance optimization) |
| **CVDM ↔ customer** | Delivers | Realizes value as a customer outcome at each journey step | Surfaces customer signal (Customer AER, 3E, experience feedback) |
