# 3E Value Measurement Model

**Efficiency | Experience | Effectiveness**

The measurement construct of the Insheights Integrated Value Creation System, developed by Insheights LLC.

The 3E Value Measurement Model is how the Insheights Integrated Value Creation System (IIVCS) measures value — from the moment it is used, through how it feels, to what it ultimately achieves. Grounded in both B2B and B2C Elements of Value, 3E provides a consistent measurement language across OSM, PTS, and CVDM — at every level, from individual value stages to the architecture elements that deliver them: Capabilities, Processes, Products/Services, and Value Streams — enabled through CVDM's five continuous feedback loops.

- **Efficiency** — measuring whether value is accessible without unnecessary friction, effort, cost, or complexity
- **Experience** — measuring whether value is being felt positively by customers and stakeholders
- **Effectiveness** — measuring whether value is delivering intended outcomes for the customer and the organization

**Product Mindset** is the operating philosophy of the IIVCS — and 3E is its measurement expression, translating the system's value promise into a customer-grounded, outcome-validated reality.

---

## The Three Dimensions

**Efficiency — Use**

The degree to which value is delivered without unnecessary friction, effort, cost, or complexity. Efficiency is the table stake of the 3E model — without it, Experience and Effectiveness cannot be reached. A customer who cannot use value without friction will not persist long enough to feel it or achieve through it.

Efficiency is grounded in the functional and operational elements of value — the elements customers and organizations need simply to engage. It is primarily a leading, operational signal.

**Experience — Feel**

The degree to which customers and stakeholders feel the value being delivered — through trust, confidence, reduced anxiety, and positive engagement. Experience is the bridge between Use and Achieve. It is the earliest actionable signal of value realization and the strongest leading indicator of whether Effectiveness will follow.

Experience is grounded in the emotional, relational, and personal elements of value — the elements that determine whether customers persist, advocate, and return. It surfaces before outcomes can be confirmed, making it the most time-sensitive dimension to measure.

**Effectiveness — Achieve**

The degree to which intended outcomes are achieved — for the customer, the organization, and the value stream. Effectiveness is the ultimate proof of value creation. It validates that the system delivered what it promised, not merely that it operated.

Effectiveness is grounded in the strategic, functional outcome, and inspirational elements of value — the elements that answer whether the value proposition was realized. It is primarily a lagging, outcome signal.

| Dimension | Sequence | Question It Answers | Grounded In | Signal Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Efficiency** | Use | Is value accessible without friction? | Functional, Operational elements | Leading — operational |
| **Experience** | Feel | Is value being felt positively? | Emotional, Relational, Personal elements | Leading — behavioral |
| **Effectiveness** | Achieve | Is value delivering intended outcomes? | Strategic, Outcome, Inspirational elements | Lagging — outcome |

---

## The Sequence — Use → Feel → Achieve

3E dimensions are not independent — they are sequenced. The sequence reflects the natural order in which value is encountered, experienced, and validated.

**Use comes first.** A customer must be able to access and use value before they can feel it. Efficiency failures at this stage prevent Experience and Effectiveness from being reached. If the system creates friction at the point of use, the customer never progresses.

**Feel comes second.** Once value is in use, the customer forms an experience — positive or negative. This feeling is the bridge to achievement. A customer who feels trust, confidence, and reduced anxiety will persist toward outcomes. A customer who feels friction, uncertainty, or disengagement will not — regardless of whether outcomes are theoretically available.

**Achieve comes third.** Outcomes are validated only after Use and Feel have been established. Effectiveness without Experience is fragile — customers who achieve outcomes but feel poor about the journey are vulnerable to churn. Effectiveness with strong Experience creates loyalty and advocacy.

```
Use  ──→  Feel  ──→  Achieve
 ↓          ↓           ↓
Efficiency  Experience  Effectiveness
(table      (bridge)    (proof)
 stake)
```

The sequence also defines the feedback priority. Experience is the first feedback item in CVDM — surfacing before outcomes can be confirmed — making it the leading signal teams act on first.

## Grounding in B2B and B2C Elements of Value

3E is grounded in Bain & Company's Elements of Value research — 30 B2C elements and 40 B2B elements spanning functional, emotional, relational, career, and inspirational dimensions. Together, the three 3E dimensions capture all 70 elements.

**Efficiency captures:**

| B2C Elements | B2B Elements |
|---|---|
| Saves time, Reduces effort, Simplifies, Avoids hassles, Reduces cost, Organizes, Integrates, Connects, Reduces risk | Time savings, Cost reduction, Decreased hassles, Simplification, Organization, Connection, Integration, Acceptable price, Meeting specs, Regulatory compliance, Ethical standards |

**Experience captures:**

| B2C Elements | B2B Elements |
|---|---|
| Reduces anxiety, Wellness, Rewards me, Nostalgia, Design/aesthetics, Badge value, Fun/entertainment, Attractiveness, Provides access, Therapeutic value, Motivation, Heirloom, Affiliation/belonging, Provides hope, Self-actualization, Self-transcendence | Reduced anxiety, Design & aesthetics, Growth & development, Perks, Commitment, Stability, Cultural fit, Expertise, Responsiveness, Fairness, Availability, Network expansion, Marketability, Reputational assurance, Hope, Social responsibility |

**Effectiveness captures:**

| B2C Elements | B2B Elements |
|---|---|
| Quality, Makes money, Informs, Variety, Sensory appeal | Product quality, Scalability, Innovation, Risk reduction, Reach, Flexibility, Component quality, Configurability, Improved topline, Vision |

No element falls outside 3E. The three dimensions provide complete coverage of what customers and organizations need from value — functionally, emotionally, and strategically.

## Application Across the IIVCS

3E applies across OSM, PTS, and CVDM. Each framework engages the dimensions it owns, reflecting the distinct role each plays in the value creation system.

**OSM** is measured for clarity, impact, and stakeholder confidence. OSM asks: are strategic outcomes being achieved, and do stakeholders have confidence in the direction?

**PTS** treats Efficiency as a table stake — a transformation that is not efficient fails before it begins. It is ultimately measured on whether the product operating model delivers the right outcomes (Effectiveness) and whether teams and customers feel the transformation positively (Experience). Efficiency is the floor; Effectiveness and Experience determine whether the transformation succeeded.

**CVDM** enables 3E. CVDM's customer journey anchor, value stream delivery, and five feedback loops are the mechanism through which Efficiency, Experience, and Effectiveness are surfaced, measured, and returned upstream to refine the system.

Each Product Mindset principle has a direct role in 3E:

| Product Mindset Principle | How It Shows Up in 3E |
|---|---|
| Customer obsession | Experience is measured from the customer's perspective first — their journey, their feeling, their outcome |
| Outcome over output | Effectiveness measures value received, not activity completed — the ultimate test of whether the system delivered |
| Continuous discovery | Experience feedback is the first and most continuous signal — teams stay in contact with how value is being felt before outcomes can be confirmed |
| Hypothesis-driven thinking | Each dimension is a testable assumption — teams define expected Efficiency, Experience, and Effectiveness, then validate against reality |
| Cross-functional ownership | 3E spans the full value delivery chain — no single function owns Efficiency, Experience, or Effectiveness in isolation |

## 3E in Practice

3E applies consistently across architecture elements, enabling comparison and decision-making across OSM, PTS, and CVDM.

| Architecture Element | Efficiency | Experience | Effectiveness |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Value Stage** | Does it activate without friction, delay, or unnecessary effort? | Does the customer feel positive, confident, and supported at this stage? | Does it deliver its intended contribution to the customer outcome? |
| **Capability** | Does it activate without waste, friction, or unnecessary cost? | Do teams and customers feel it working positively? | Does it deliver its intended contribution to the value stage? |
| **Process** | Does it execute without friction, delay, or unnecessary effort? | Do participants feel it is fair, clear, and supportive? | Does it produce its intended outcome consistently? |
| **Product/Service** | Can customers access and use it without friction? | Do customers feel positive, confident, and supported? | Does it deliver the intended value proposition? |
| **Value Stream** | Does it activate and deliver without unnecessary cost or friction? | Do customers and teams experience it positively? | Does it achieve its strategic outcomes? |

This consistency gives OSM, PTS, and CVDM a shared measurement language for portfolio decisions — which Capabilities to invest in, which Processes to improve, which Products/Services to continue or sunset, and which Value Streams to prioritize or rebalance.

## Summary

3E measures value the way customers experience it — in sequence, across dimensions, and grounded in what actually matters to them.

Efficiency ensures value can be used. Experience ensures value is felt. Effectiveness ensures value is achieved. Together, they form the complete measurement language of the Insheights Integrated Value Creation System — from strategic intent in OSM, through transformation in PTS, to delivery and feedback in CVDM.

The feedback runs upstream — Experience surfacing first, Effectiveness confirming last, and both returning signal that continuously refines where and how the system creates value.
