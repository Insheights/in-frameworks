# IIVCS Detailed Assessment

**A structured, evidence-anchored, gated self-assessment across all IIVCS components — producing a maturity profile that identifies strengths, gaps, and adoption priorities. The full IIVCS Assessment Instrument.**

---

This instrument is the scored counterpart to the [Adoption Sequencing & Maturity Model](adoption-maturity.md). Where the Maturity Model is the qualitative map — what early, developing, and mature adoption looks like, and where to start — this instrument **places your organization on that map** with an evidence-anchored, gated reading across every IIVCS component.

- The [Quick Self-Assessment](quick-assessment.md) gives a directional reading in minutes (one question per element).
- **This instrument** gives the rigorous reading: leveled, evidence-anchored criteria across 8 components, scored by *gating* (not averaging) into a maturity profile.

> **Assess one value stream at a time.** IIVCS maturity is about how the frameworks connect to deliver value — and they connect *on a value stream*. Run this instrument against a single, named value stream end-to-end for a true reading. Assessing "the whole organization" at once averages away exactly the connection gaps this instrument exists to find.

Read [What This Instrument Is — and Is Not](#what-this-instrument-is--and-is-not) before quoting a result.

---

## How to Use It

The assessment is run by **several respondents who score independently first, then reconcile** — not by one leader, and not by a room scoring as a single voice. Independent-first scoring is deliberate: it prevents anchoring and groupthink, and it is what lets the assessment capture genuinely different perspectives across the IIVCS elements. Divergence between respondents is signal, not noise.

1. **Choose the value stream** and assemble the respondents (see [Who Participates](#who-participates)).
2. **Score independently first.** Each respondent rates the criteria they have visibility into — ✓ / ~ / ✗, or **—** (no visibility) — recording evidence. No group discussion yet.
3. **Collate and reconcile.** Line up the individual ratings per criterion. Unanimous ✓ *with evidence* → ✓. Any divergence → ⚡ Split: discuss the evidence to resolve it, or record it as a finding — *who* saw it differently (e.g., leadership vs. the doers) is itself diagnostic.
4. **Gate each component to a level** using the rule below — a level is earned only when *all* its criteria (and all criteria below it) are ✓. Averages are not used.
5. **Read system maturity** as the weakest *connected* link, **carry the dispersion log**, and **build the maturity profile** — strengths, constraint, adoption priority.

> **Two ways to run steps 2–3.** *Facilitated (recommended):* respondents score privately, then meet to reconcile live — fastest path to resolving Splits. *Async-aggregate:* collect individual sheets and aggregate without a live meeting — lighter and good for distributed teams, but unresolved divergences simply remain as Split findings. Either way, **score privately before comparing** — that is the part that protects the result.

### Response options

Each respondent rates every visible criterion with **exactly one** of the four options below. The definitions are deliberately strict and mutually exclusive — the instrument is built to reveal the true state, not the intended one, so the options are written to stop "rounding up."

| Option | Use it when… | Counts toward the gate? |
|---|---|---|
| **✓ Met** | The criterion is true **across the value stream**, is **the normal way of working**, and a respondent can **point to the evidence** named in the criterion's Evidence column | **Yes** |
| **~ Partial** | The thing genuinely exists but falls short on **consistency**, **maturity**, or **connection** — note which | No — it is your next move within the component |
| **✗ Not** | There is **no instance**, it happens only by **individual initiative with no design behind it**, or it exists only as a **plan, tool, or intention** | No |
| **— No visibility** | This respondent **cannot see** this element | Not a score — excluded from this respondent's ratings; never guess to fill a gap |

> **The Met (✓) test — all three must hold:**
> 1. **Consistent** — true across the value stream, not in one team or one instance.
> 2. **Sustained** — the normal, repeatable way of working — not a pilot, a recent start, or a one-off.
> 3. **Evidenced** — a respondent can point to the artifact, behavior, or metric in the Evidence column.
>
> Missing any one of the three → **~ Partial**, not ✓.

**Decision rules that keep responses honest:**

- **When torn between two options, choose the lower.** "Almost Met" is Partial; "barely Partial" is Not.
- **One instance is not Met.** A pilot, a single team, or a recent start is **~ Partial** at most.
- **"We intend to / we're planning to / we have a tool for it" is ✗ Not** — capability is not practice.
- **No evidence, no ✓.** If no one can point to where it is true, it is not Met, regardless of belief.

> **⚡ Split** is not an option a respondent picks — it is what *collation* produces when respondents diverge on the same criterion (e.g., leadership rates ✓, the doers rate ✗). It never counts toward the gate, and the divergence is itself a finding. See [How to Use It](#how-to-use-it).

### A note on quantitative thresholds

Some criteria below are marked **†** — they depend on a numeric threshold (a coverage %, a cadence, a "consistently") that the [Measurement Framework — Metrics and Indicators](ROADMAP.md) guide will define. Until that guide is published, score **†** criteria on the room's evidence-based judgment and **record the basis you used** (e.g., "AER measured on 3 of the value stream's 4 primary journeys"). When thresholds are published, re-score these against them — your recorded basis makes that re-score honest.

## Who Participates

This is a **cross-functional, multi-respondent assessment for one value stream — not one leader scoring alone.** Each participant scores independently first, then the group reconciles (see [How to Use It](#how-to-use-it)). The assessment exists to find where the frameworks *connect* (strategy → operating model → delivery → feedback), and any single role sees only its own layer — so capturing *different perspectives across the elements* is part of the method, and divergence between roles is a finding, not a problem. The set of respondents must span the layers, and must include people close to the actual work — not only leadership — or the scores drift aspirational.

| Participant | What they bring | Components they primarily inform |
|---|---|---|
| **Facilitator** (neutral) | Runs the session and holds the evidence standard — "show me where this is true" — so scores reflect reality, not intent | All |
| **Value-stream owner** | Owns the value stream being assessed; the anchor participant | OSM; all |
| **Strategy / governance lead** (or executive sponsor) | Knows the strategic priorities and how governance actually operates | OSM, 8 Values, Feedback System |
| **Product / delivery lead** | Knows team structure, POM Enabler activation, and the lifecycle | PTS, Product Mindset |
| **Cross-functional team member(s)** | Close to the actual work — the honesty anchor against aspirational scoring | PTS, CVDM, Product Mindset |
| **Customer-facing team member** | Knows the journey as customers experience it and the signal it produces | CVDM, AER |
| **Data / analytics support** | Knows what is *actually* measured today versus what is assumed | 3E, AER, 8 Values |

**Principles for the room:**

- **Span the layers, keep it small.** Aim for 4–7 people covering strategy, operating model, delivery, and measurement. Bigger rooms score slower and no truer.
- **Include the doers, not just the deciders.** Leadership tends to score what was intended; the people doing the work score what is real. You need both, and the gap between them is a ⚡ Split — itself a finding.
- **One voice does not own a score.** Each criterion is agreed from evidence; a lone role asserting a ✓ is a ⚡ Split.
- **The facilitator should be neutral** — ideally not the value-stream owner — so no one is scoring their own homework.

## The Assessment

For each component, criteria are grouped by the level they unlock. **Developing criteria** must all be Met to clear *Early*; **Mature criteria** must all be Met *in addition* to clear *Developing*. Mark each ✓ / ~ / ✗ / ⚡ and note the evidence.

### 1. OSM — Strategy & Governance

**Developing criteria** *(all must be ✓ to be at least Developing)*

| # | Criterion | Evidence that it is Met | Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.D1 | Strategic priorities are expressed as outcomes, not activities or initiatives | A written priority list where each item names an outcome and a success measure — not a deliverable | |
| 1.D2 | Each priority maps to a value stream with a named owner | A priority-to-value-stream map with named owners | |
| 1.D3 | A governance cadence reviews whether priorities are generating their intended outcomes | A recurring governance forum with minutes referencing outcome progress | |

| # | Criterion | Evidence that it is Met | Rating |

| 1.M2 | Dual-track AER and the 8 Values are reconciled in governance | A governance pack showing Org AER + Customer AER + 8 Values read together against priorities | |
| 1.M3 | Value-stream outcomes are defined and owned distinctly from delivery output | Documented value-stream outcomes, separate from team output metrics | |

## Scoring — Gated, Not Averaged

Maturity is **gated**: a component earns a level only when *all* the criteria at that level and below are Met (✓). This is deliberately non-compensatory — a strength on one criterion cannot buy back a gap on another, because maturity is about not having the gap.

**Gate each component:**

| Component level | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Mature** | All Developing criteria ✓ **and** all Mature criteria ✓ |
| **Developing** | All Developing criteria ✓ (but not all Mature criteria ✓) |
| **Early** | Not all Developing criteria are ✓ |

Any **~ Partial** or **⚡ Split** on a criterion means that criterion is *not* ✓ — so it does not clear the gate. Record it: Partials are your next move within the component; Splits are a disagreement to resolve before the score is trustworthy.

**System maturity** is **not** an average across components. It is the level of your **weakest connected link** along the chain strategy → operating model → delivery → feedback (components **1 → 2 → 3 → 7**). A system is only as connected as its weakest link. The measurement and foundation components (4, 5, 6, 8) are read as *enablers* of that chain, not as independent scores (see the note in the profile).

## Your Maturity Profile

Transfer each component's gated level and note the criteria holding it back.

In the third column, list every criterion in that component that did **not** reach ✓ Met — these are exactly what keep the component below the next level. A criterion falls short of ✓ when it was rated:

- **~ Partial** — it exists but is not yet consistent, sustained, *and* connected (fails the [Met test](#response-options));
- **⚡ Split** — respondents diverged, so it is not a confirmed ✓ (and the disagreement is itself a finding);
- **✗ Not** — it is absent, ad hoc, or an intention only.

Listing these turns the score into a to-do list — each shortfall is a concrete next move.

| Component | Level (Early/Developing/Mature) | Criteria blocking the next level *(list each, with its ~ / ⚡ / ✗ rating)* |
|---|---|---|
| 1. OSM — Strategy & Governance | | |
| 2. PTS — Operating Model | | |
| 3. CVDM — Delivery & Realization | | |
| 4. 3E — Operational Measurement | | |
| 5. AER — Behavioral Measurement | | |
| 6. 8 Values — Organizational Outcomes | | |
| 7. Feedback System — Upstream Loops | | |
| 8. Product Mindset — Foundation | | |
| **System maturity** *(weakest connected link, 1→2→3→7)* | | |

**Dispersion log** — from the independent ratings, record every **⚡ Split** and the *pattern*: which role rated higher and which lower, not just that they disagreed. A consistent leadership-rates-higher pattern means strategy or the operating model is not landing where the work happens; a cluster of Splits in one component means you do not yet have a reliable reading there — resolve them before acting on that component's level. The by-role divergence is often the most actionable output of the whole assessment.

**Then read three things:**

- **Strengths** — components at Mature. Protect these; they are where the system already works.
- **Constraint** — your lowest *connected* component (1, 2, 3, or 7). This is the break where value leaks out — and your adoption priority. The specific criteria *not yet ✓* are your concrete next moves.
- **Entry point & sequence** — map your constraint to a starting point below, then follow the [sequencing principles](adoption-maturity.md#sequencing-principles) in the Maturity Model.

| If your constraint is… | Start with | Then |
|---|---|---|
| OSM (1) — strategy unclear, priorities not governed as outcomes | **OSM** | PTS → CVDM |
| PTS (2) — strategy clear but delivery is projects-and-handoffs | **PTS** | CVDM → formalize OSM |
| CVDM (3) — acute journey / retention / delivery problem | **CVDM** | PTS → OSM |
| Feedback System (7) — frameworks run but signal does not flow upstream | **the weakest of OSM/PTS/CVDM feeding it** | then close the loop CVDM → PTS → OSM |

> **A note on the measurement and foundation components (4, 5, 6, 8).** These are not separate entry points — they are how the three frameworks are instrumented and operated. A gap here usually *explains* a gap on the framework it serves: a weak 3E (4) shows up as a blind CVDM (3); a weak Product Mindset (8) shows up across all three. Close them in service of the connected framework, not in isolation.

## Reassessing

Maturity is a moving state, not a milestone. Re-run this instrument at the same cadence as your strategic governance review (typically quarterly), against the same value stream, to track movement.

- **Watch the *system maturity* level move — not an average.** A rising system level means the frameworks are becoming more connected; isolated component gains do not move it.
- **Re-score † criteria when the Metrics and Indicators thresholds are published** — using the basis you recorded, so the re-score is honest.
- **Resolve Splits between runs.** A criterion that is ⚡ Split twice is a standing disagreement about reality — not a scoring problem.
- **When one value stream reaches Mature, assess the next.** Maturity spreads value stream by value stream, not in a single organization-wide leap.

## What This Instrument Is — and Is Not

Stated plainly, so results are not over-claimed:

- **It is** a structured, evidence-anchored, gated judgment instrument. Its rigor comes from gating (non-compensatory levels), evidence anchors per criterion, explicit dispersion capture, and a cross-functional room — not from statistical validation.
- **It is not** a psychometrically validated or externally benchmarked measure. It has not been calibrated against a population of known-mature and known-immature organizations.
- **Some thresholds are not yet fixed.** Criteria marked † depend on the [Metrics and Indicators](ROADMAP.md) guide; until then they rest on recorded, evidence-based judgment.
- **Its reliability depends on the room.** Honest evidence, the doers present, and a neutral facilitator are what make a result trustworthy. Run without them, it measures optimism.
- **Use the result to act, not to certify.** It is built to find your constraint and direct your next move — a directional grade for decisions, not a certified score for external reporting.
