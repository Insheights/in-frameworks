# Insheights 3E Operational Values Measurement Model — Comparison with Industry Standards

**ISO 9241-11 | Balanced Scorecard | Google HEART | NPS / CSAT / CES | AARRR**

A comparison of the Insheights 3E Operational Values Measurement Model against established usability, enterprise measurement, product analytics, and customer experience frameworks.

---

## Positioning

3E (Efficiency | Experience | Effectiveness) measures value delivery in sequence — from whether value can be accessed (Use), through whether it is felt positively (Feel), to whether it achieves intended outcomes (Achieve). It is applied consistently across every architecture element in the IIVCS: Value Streams, Value Stages, Capabilities, Processes, and Products/Services.

The frameworks below each capture a portion of what 3E measures. None sequences its dimensions as a causal chain, and none applies a single measurement language consistently from individual process level to full value stream level while grounding every dimension in customer value elements.

## Comparison by Framework

### ISO 9241-11 (Usability: Effectiveness, Efficiency, Satisfaction)

ISO 9241-11 is the international ergonomics standard for usability, defining it through three dimensions:

- **Effectiveness** — the accuracy and completeness with which users achieve specified goals
- **Efficiency** — the resources expended in relation to accuracy and completeness of goal achievement
- **Satisfaction** — freedom from discomfort and positive attitudes toward the use of the product

**Alignment:** The structural parallel is direct. 3E shares two of three terms and maps the third — Satisfaction becomes Experience. Both frameworks position Efficiency as a resource-and-friction measure, a third dimension as an experiential measure, and the final dimension as outcome achievement. 3E names the same underlying insight: that value delivery can be measured across use, feel, and achieve.

**Key difference:** ISO 9241-11 is a product-interaction standard applied at the task and session level — measuring whether a specific user can complete a specific task with a specific product. 3E is an organizational value measurement model applied across the full value delivery architecture, from individual Process level to Value Stream level. Where ISO 9241 operationalizes usability with task completion rates, error frequencies, and time-on-task, 3E measures whether value streams, capabilities, and products are delivering intended outcomes for customers and organizations.

The most significant structural difference is sequencing. ISO 9241-11 treats its three dimensions as parallel, independent lenses applied simultaneously to a product interaction. 3E sequences them — Efficiency is the table stake, Experience is the bridge, and Effectiveness is the proof — making the model a diagnostic tool, not just a measurement taxonomy.

**3E gap filled:** ISO 9241-11 has no causal model between its dimensions, no connection to behavioral outcomes (AER) or organizational value (8 Values), and no coverage beyond the product-interaction level. It is a precision instrument for a specific layer; 3E is a consistent language for the full system.

### Balanced Scorecard (Kaplan & Norton)

The Balanced Scorecard organizes measurement into four perspectives — Financial, Customer, Internal Process, and Learning & Growth — to give leaders a balanced view of organizational performance beyond financial metrics alone.

**Alignment:** Partial structural mapping exists. The Customer perspective captures elements of Experience and Effectiveness — how customers perceive the organization and whether they are satisfied. The Internal Process perspective captures elements of Efficiency — how well operations run. Learning & Growth captures the organizational capability dimension that 3E addresses through Effectiveness at the Capability and Process level.

**Key difference:** The Balanced Scorecard's four perspectives are parallel and simultaneous — the model gives leaders four lenses to watch at once, each weighted by strategic importance. 3E's three dimensions are sequential and causal — the sequence is the diagnostic model. When Effectiveness underperforms in 3E, the framework directs the practitioner to check Experience first, then Efficiency. The Balanced Scorecard's strategy maps do assert causal links between perspectives, but they provide no diagnostic sequence for where to look first when an outcome underperforms — the practitioner reads state across four perspectives and infers the cause.

The Balanced Scorecard is designed for strategic management of the whole organization; 3E is a value measurement model that applies at every architecture level from Value Stream to Process. A BSC practitioner watches four perspectives at the organizational level; a 3E practitioner applies three questions to any named element in the delivery system.

**3E gap filled:** The Balanced Scorecard has no grounding in customer value elements — its Customer perspective captures perception and satisfaction broadly but does not map to a taxonomy of what customers and organizations actually value. Its leading/lagging logic — performance drivers ahead of outcomes — is real but is constructed per strategy map and left to the practitioner to define; it is not a structural property of the model the way 3E classifies Efficiency and Experience as leading and Effectiveness as lagging at every architecture element.

### Google HEART Framework

HEART (Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task Success) is a product analytics framework developed at Google for measuring user experience at scale. It provides five dimensions with corresponding signal types (Goals, Signals, Metrics) that teams define per product.

**Alignment:** HEART maps closely to 3E at the Product/Service level. Adoption and Task Success map to Efficiency — they measure whether users can access and successfully use value. Happiness maps to Experience — how users feel about the product. Engagement and Retention map to Effectiveness outcomes — whether users continue and deepen their use over time.

**Key difference:** HEART is a product analytics instrumentation framework — it tells teams what categories of metrics to define for a specific product. 3E is a value measurement architecture — it tells practitioners what sequence to apply across any element in the delivery system, why that sequence matters causally, and how the measurement connects forward to behavioral response (AER) and organizational value (8 Values). HEART has no causal model between its dimensions; the five categories are parallel choices, not a diagnostic sequence. A product with low Retention in HEART does not instruct the practitioner to check Happiness before checking Adoption. 3E does.

HEART also has no cross-architectural consistency — it applies to products, not to the capabilities, processes, and value streams that surround and support them.

**3E gap filled:** HEART has no causal sequencing between dimensions, no connection to organizational or behavioral outcomes, and no coverage beyond the product analytics layer. It is a strong instrumentation framework for a single level of the architecture; 3E provides the measurement language for the full system that product sits within.

### NPS / CSAT / CES

Net Promoter Score (NPS), Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT), and Customer Effort Score (CES) are the three dominant single-dimension customer measurement instruments in commercial use.

- **CES** measures the effort required to accomplish a specific task — "How easy was it to [complete action]?" It is a leading signal of friction at the point of use.
- **CSAT** measures satisfaction with a specific interaction or experience — "How satisfied were you with [interaction]?" It is a point-in-time Experience signal.
- **NPS** measures stated advocacy likelihood — "How likely are you to recommend?" It is used as a proxy for Effectiveness and loyalty.

**Alignment:** Each instrument maps to one dimension of 3E. CES maps to Efficiency — it measures the friction that 3E's Efficiency dimension captures. CSAT maps to Experience — it captures the feeling dimension at a specific interaction. NPS maps partially to Effectiveness — it measures stated advocacy as a proxy for value realization.

**Key difference:** NPS, CSAT, and CES are single-dimension instruments deployed independently — they capture one dimension of value delivery at one moment in time. 3E is a complete measurement model that applies all three dimensions together, in sequence, across any element in the delivery architecture. Standard practice deploys these instruments independently, producing fragmented signals with no causal model connecting them. 3E is the architecture that makes them coherent: CES is an Efficiency instrument, CSAT is an Experience instrument, and NPS is a partial Effectiveness instrument — together, they are the signal layer for the 3E model.

The most significant limitation of all three is the absence of a causal model. A team that sees a declining NPS does not know from that signal whether the cause is an Efficiency failure (friction preventing use), an Experience failure (poor feeling undermining confidence), or a genuine Effectiveness failure (outcomes not materializing). 3E provides the diagnostic logic that single-instrument approaches cannot.

**3E gap filled:** No causal sequence, no cross-architectural application, no grounding in customer value elements, no connection to behavioral response or organizational outcomes. Each instrument captures one valid signal; 3E connects them into a complete measurement model.

### AARRR (Pirate Metrics)

AARRR (Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Referral → Revenue) is a growth framework for digital products and businesses, sequencing the stages a customer moves through from first awareness to revenue generation.

**Alignment:** AARRR is the closest industry analogue to 3E's sequencing principle. Activation maps to Efficiency — the customer can access and use the product without friction. Retention and Referral map to Effectiveness — the customer continues and advocates because value was delivered. Revenue is the financial Effectiveness signal.

**Key difference:** AARRR is a company-centric growth funnel — it sequences the stages from the organization's perspective (acquire customers, get them activated, retain them, generate revenue). 3E is customer-outcome-centric — it sequences the customer's value realization experience (access value, feel it, achieve through it). AARRR has no Experience dimension — it moves from Activation directly to Retention without accounting for the quality of what the customer felt in between. This is precisely the gap 3E names as the bridge: customers who are activated but do not feel value positively will not persist to Retention regardless of what the product delivers.

AARRR also applies only to the customer acquisition and growth funnel, not to the full delivery architecture. A capability gap or process failure that undermines value delivery does not appear in AARRR until Retention or Revenue degrades — there is no upstream diagnostic layer.

**3E gap filled:** No Experience dimension between Activation and Retention, no causal diagnostic model, no cross-architectural application, no grounding in customer value elements, no connection to organizational outcomes.

## The Distinctive Contribution of 3E

### Sequencing as a Diagnostic Tool

No established measurement framework treats its dimensions as a causal sequence and uses that sequence as a diagnostic model. ISO 9241-11, Balanced Scorecard, and HEART all present their dimensions as parallel lenses — the practitioner reads all of them simultaneously and draws conclusions. 3E's sequence — Efficiency is the table stake, Experience is the bridge, Effectiveness is the proof — is not just an organizing principle; it is the instruction for where to look when something is wrong. An Effectiveness gap always requires checking Experience before concluding. An Experience gap always requires checking Efficiency before acting. The sequence makes root cause analysis structural rather than practitioner-dependent.

### Leading and Lagging Signal Classification

3E explicitly classifies its dimensions by signal timing: Efficiency and Experience are leading (operational and behavioral signals that surface before outcomes materialize), and Effectiveness is lagging (outcome validation that surfaces last). This classification is unusual in measurement frameworks — most mix leading and lagging metrics without naming the distinction. The consequence is operational: teams monitoring leading signals can identify value failures before they register as outcome degradation. The NPS decline, the Retention drop, the revenue shortfall — by the time these Effectiveness signals appear, the upstream failure has usually been present for weeks or months. 3E's leading signal structure is designed to surface intervention opportunities before lagging indicators confirm the damage.

### Complete Coverage via Elements of Value

3E is grounded in Bain & Company's Elements of Value research — 30 B2C elements and 40 B2B elements spanning functional, emotional, relational, career, and inspirational dimensions. The three 3E dimensions collectively capture all 70 elements, with no element falling outside the model. This grounding means 3E is not an arbitrary three-category framework — each dimension covers a defined set of customer value drivers, and together they achieve complete coverage of what customers and organizations need from value delivery. Among the frameworks compared here, 3E is distinctive in grounding its dimensions in an external, researched taxonomy of customer value rather than in author-defined categories.

### Cross-Architectural Consistency

3E applies the same three questions — Efficiency, Experience, Effectiveness — from Value Stream level down to Process level, giving every element in the IIVCS a shared measurement language. A portfolio decision comparing two Value Streams uses the same language as a team improving a single Process. This consistency makes cross-level comparison and root cause tracing possible in a way that frameworks designed for a single level (HEART for products, AARRR for growth funnels, ISO 9241-11 for interactions) cannot support.

### Connection to Behavioral and Organizational Outcomes

3E does not terminate at measurement — it connects forward to AER (behavioral response) and upstream to the 8 Values (organizational outcomes). Each 3E dimension drives the corresponding AER stage: Efficiency enables Adoption, Experience sustains Engagement, Effectiveness earns Retention. AER in turn drives the 8 Values — Customer, Financial, People, Operational, and the compounding Values over time. No established measurement framework embeds itself within a connected outcome architecture in this way. NPS, BSC, and HEART all terminate at the signal they produce; 3E is a node in a connected system.
