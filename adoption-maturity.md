# IIVCS Adoption Sequencing & Maturity Model

**Where to start · What sequence to follow · What early, developing, and mature adoption looks like across the suite**

---

This model answers the two questions every organization asks before adopting the IIVCS:

1. **Where do we start** — OSM, PTS, or CVDM — and in what sequence?
2. **How mature are we** — what does early, developing, and mature adoption look like, and what would it take to advance?

It is a self-orientation tool. For a directional reading use the [IIVCS Quick Self-Assessment](quick-assessment.md); for a structured, scored evaluation use the [IIVCS Detailed Assessment](detail-assessment.md). This model is the map that makes either result actionable: it tells you where you are and what to do next.

> **The core idea:** IIVCS maturity is not the average of how good each framework is in isolation. It is how well the frameworks are **connected as a system** — strategy governing an operating model that realizes value at the customer journey, with signal flowing back upstream. An organization can run a strong operating model and still be *early* as a system if nothing connects it to strategy or customer reality.

---

## The Three Maturity Levels

Adoption maturity is defined at three levels across the suite. The levels describe the **system**, not any single framework.

| Level | What it looks like across the suite |
|---|---|
| **Early** | One or two components operate in isolation. Measurement is activity- or output-based. There is no upstream feedback loop — signals die at the team. Frameworks, if present, are not connected to each other. |
| **Developing** | Multiple components are connected on at least one value stream. 3E is applied in places; one AER track (usually customer) is measured. Some feedback loops run and reach PTS. Strategy, operating model, and delivery are partially linked but not yet a closed system. |
| **Mature** | All three frameworks operate as one connected system across value streams. Dual-track AER (Customer + Organizational) is measured in parallel; the 8 Values aggregate from their operational source; the full upstream feedback loop (CVDM → PTS → OSM) revises strategy on signal. The system is self-correcting. |

## Maturity Across the Suite

Read each row and find the column that best describes you today. Your **system maturity is closest to your weakest connected row** — a chain is only as strong as its links, and the IIVCS is a chain.

| Component | Early | Developing | Mature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OSM** — Strategy & Governance | Strategy is a deck; priorities are activities, not outcomes; no value-stream governance | Value priorities stated as outcomes and mapped to some value streams; governance cadence exists but rarely revises priorities | Priorities governed by value stream; Governance & Measurement reconciles dual-track AER + 8 Values and revises strategy on signal |
| **PTS** — Operating Model | Project-based delivery; functional silos and handoffs; teams disband per initiative | Some persistent cross-functional teams on key value streams; partial POM Enabler activation | Persistent value-stream teams; all four POM Enablers active; continuous lifecycle; Organizational AER measured |
| **CVDM** — Delivery & Realization | Delivery organized by internal process, not the customer journey; no journey-to-value-stream map | Journey mapped to value stream on key journeys; some value stages defined; a few feedback loops running | Full journey-to-value-stream mapping; value stages defined as outcomes; all five loops active; Customer AER measured |
| **3E** — Operational Measurement | Metrics are activity/output; no consistent measurement language | 3E applied at some elements; Use → Feel → Achieve read in places | 3E applied consistently across architecture levels and read in sequence diagnostically |
| **AER** — Behavioral Measurement | No behavioral measurement; satisfaction surveys at best | One track measured (usually Customer AER) on some journeys | Dual-track AER (Customer + Organizational) measured in parallel and reconciled at OSM |
| **8 Values** — Organizational Outcomes | Value measured only financially/operationally, in silos | Several value dimensions tracked; some traceable to an operational source | All 8 Values aggregated from their operational source and feeding OSM governance |
| **Feedback System** — Upstream Loops | No upstream feedback; signals die at the team | Operational loops run and reach PTS; signal informs delivery | Full upstream path CVDM → PTS → OSM; operational + organizational feedback drives strategic reassessment |
| **Product Mindset** — Foundation | Output-focused, project culture; assumptions go untested | Outcome language adopted; discovery practiced in places | Customer obsession, outcome-over-output, continuous discovery, hypothesis-driven thinking, and cross-functional ownership are the norm |

> **How to read your result:** Note the column for each row. A row sits at a level only when it has *fully* reached it — the scored [Detailed Assessment](detail-assessment.md) makes this explicit by *gating* (you earn a level by meeting all its criteria, not by averaging close to it). Scattered results (some Mature, some Early) are the most common and the most useful finding — they show exactly which connection is missing. The component holding you back is your next adoption priority.

## Self-Assessment

Two assessment artifacts place you on this model. Use whichever fits the moment:

- **[IIVCS Quick Self-Assessment](quick-assessment.md)** — a directional reading in a few minutes: one question per IIVCS element, each answer mapping to Early / Developing / Mature. Start here.
- **[IIVCS Detailed Assessment](detail-assessment.md)** — the rigorous, scored evaluation: 32 statements across all eight components, banded into a full maturity profile with strengths, constraint, and adoption priority.

Both ask the same thing the grid above does — *where is each element, and where is the weakest connected link?* — and both point back here for what to do next.

> **Assess one value stream at a time.** IIVCS maturity is about how the frameworks connect to deliver value — and they connect *on a value stream*. Assess a single, named value stream end-to-end, not the whole organization at once.

### Assessment is periodic, not one-time

Maturity is a moving state, not a milestone. Assess on a recurring **cadence** — re-run the assessment against the same value stream at the rhythm of your strategic governance review (typically quarterly), so movement is visible and the result keeps driving action. A one-time score tells you where you were; a repeated one tells you whether the system is actually becoming more connected.

- **Same value stream, same questions, regular interval** — so each reading is comparable to the last.
- **Watch the *system maturity* band move, not the average** — a rising band means the frameworks are connecting; a flat band with a rising average means components are improving in isolation.
- **Re-run out of cycle** after any significant change — a strategy reset, an operating-model change, or a major delivery shift can move maturity up or down, and the assessment should confirm which.
- **When one value stream reaches Mature, assess the next** — maturity spreads value stream by value stream, not in a single organization-wide leap.

## Adoption Sequencing — Where to Start

The IIVCS is designed so **OSM governs, PTS operationalizes, CVDM realizes**, with feedback running upstream (CVDM → PTS → OSM). That gives a canonical top-down sequence — but you start where your pain is, then backfill. There are three valid entry points.

### Entry point 1 — Strategy-led (start with OSM)

**Start here when:** strategic intent is unclear, value priorities are contested, or execution is drifting from intent. The break is between strategy and everything below it.

**Sequence:** OSM → PTS → CVDM. Define value priorities and value-stream outcomes first, build the operating model to deliver them, then wire the customer journey to realize them.

### Entry point 2 — Operating-model-led (start with PTS)

**Start here when:** strategy is reasonably clear but delivery is broken — projects not products, handoffs, no value-stream ownership. The break is between intent and execution.

**Sequence:** PTS → CVDM → formalize OSM. Stand up persistent value-stream teams and the operating model around existing priorities, push into CVDM at the journey, then formalize OSM governance as the feedback loop matures.

### Entry point 3 — Delivery-led (start with CVDM)

**Start here when:** there is an acute, visible customer problem — a broken journey, failing retention — and you need a fast proof of value. The break is at the point of delivery.

**Sequence:** CVDM → PTS → OSM. Fix one customer journey end-to-end on a single value stream, then pull upstream — using CVDM's feedback loops to inform the operating model (PTS) and strategy (OSM).

| If your situation is… | Start with | Then |
|---|---|---|
| Strategy unclear / execution drifting from intent | **OSM** | PTS → CVDM |
| Strategy clear, delivery broken (projects, handoffs) | **PTS** | CVDM → formalize OSM |
| Acute customer / journey / retention problem | **CVDM** | PTS → OSM |

---

## Sequencing Principles

Whatever the entry point, four principles govern a healthy adoption:

- **Define before operationalize before realize.** Outcomes should be defined (OSM) before the operating model is built (PTS) before delivery is wired (CVDM). Starting downstream is valid — but you will have to backfill the upstream definition, or the work has no anchor.
- **Start narrow.** Adopt on a *single value stream*, end-to-end, before scaling across the suite. One connected value stream proves the system; ten disconnected pilots prove nothing.
- **Measurement follows function.** Apply 3E as soon as one architecture element exists; add AER once delivery and the operating model run; aggregate the 8 Values once the system produces outcomes to read. Do not stand up a board scorecard before the underlying signals exist.
- **Feedback matures last.** The full upstream loop (CVDM → PTS → OSM) is the final thing to reach maturity, because it requires all three frameworks operating and connected. It is the signature of a mature system, not a starting condition.

> **Recommended default path:** Pilot on one value stream — define its outcome (OSM), stand up a persistent team and operating model for it (PTS), wire its customer journey (CVDM) — then activate the feedback loops and only then scale to the next value stream. Top-down to establish the anchor; bottom-up feedback to keep it honest.

## Connection to the Assessments

This model is the qualitative map; the assessments place an organization on it. The [IIVCS Detailed Assessment](detail-assessment.md) spans all IIVCS components — OSM, PTS, CVDM, 3E, AER, 8 Values, the Feedback System, and Product Mindset — and produces a maturity profile identifying strengths, gaps, and adoption priorities.

The pieces work together:

- The **[Quick Self-Assessment](quick-assessment.md)** and **[Detailed Assessment](detail-assessment.md)** tell you *where you are* — directionally, or as a scored profile across the components above.
- This **model** tells you *what to do next* — which level you are at, which connection is your constraint, and where to start.

## Summary

- IIVCS maturity is measured by **connection**, not by any single framework's strength in isolation.
- The three levels — **Early, Developing, Mature** — describe the system: from isolated components, to partial connection on a value stream, to a self-correcting system with a closed upstream feedback loop.
- There are **three valid entry points** — strategy-led (OSM), operating-model-led (PTS), or delivery-led (CVDM) — chosen by where the break is, then backfilled toward a connected system.
- Adopt **one value stream end-to-end first**, let measurement follow function, and treat the full upstream feedback loop as the signature of maturity.
- **Assess on a cadence, not once.** Re-run the assessment against the same value stream at your governance rhythm — maturity is a moving state, and the point is to watch the system maturity band move over time.

See [Framework Positioning](positioning.md) for how the three frameworks relate, and the [Feedback System](feedback.md) for the upstream loop that defines mature adoption.
